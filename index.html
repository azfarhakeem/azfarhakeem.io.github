magic mirror

A smart home device which is integrated inside a Raspberry Pi 3 using java script coding connected to a display monitor behind a two-way mirror. Magic Mirror is an open source platform available to use on github.The smart mirror displays a few information ranging from time, weather, calendar, prayer time, travel time and game results such as football and formula 1. The mirror has also been integrated to work with Alexa and Google Photos.

Smart home

Me and family are much interested in technology especially smart home device. However, installation and smart home system are expensive. So we decided to installed and build it ourself using cheap wifi switched called Sonoff. We would attach this switch into every wiring in the house including lighting, air conditionings and other home devices. While some we use universal IR blaster to turn on our TVs, Fans and also the air conditioning. I also installed wifi motor switches to our curtains so that they can open in the morning and close for the night. For software, I linked all the switches with Amazon Alexa app so that we can remote and even voice control everything thorugh Alexa.

floating aquarium

Designed a floating aquarium which the purpose is to let fish swim up to the elevated aquarium. The design is designed using solidworks and then the design is given to a perspex workshop to fabricate the design. During installation, air from inside the aquarium is sucked out, causing pressure form outside to push the water inside and upwards creating a 'floating affect'.

lab rover

The need of a reliable and effective remote laboratory system due to dangerous laboratory setup, social distancing (COVID19) and inefficient system has paved the way for this project. It consists of a viewing system which can replicate the upper human body movement as well as being able to charge itself and available 24/7. It is modelled using SOLIDWORKS and created using aluminium profiles and 3D printed. The system also consists of lithium-ion battery pack which is spot welded into a 4s2p configuration with a voltage of 16.8V.

it is also equipped with a DIY charger and programmed so that the system can charge itself after use. The robot is controlled using arduino IDE which uses C++ to give commands to each respective motors.

This project is a continuous project which is later pass on to the upcoming 4th year student to continue progress on creating the wireless charging system and auto home.

Japan Super Science Fair

I attended the 2014 Japan Super Science Fair (JSSF) in Kyoto as a presenter and participant. At the fair, I was given the opportunity to present my project on an international platform. I presented my project, a Bluetooth virtual leash that prevents valuable items from getting lost due to theft or misplacement. Equally valuable was the opportunity to listen to the other project presentations which were very inspiring. The science fair activity that I took part in allowed me to share ideas with other international students on a mini roller coaster design. The roller coaster worked upon the force of gravity. Therefore, in order to get the most journey time, we designed the plane to have the lowest angle to move at optimum speed. We thoughtfully applied physics knowledge and won first place in that competition.

Robotics

I was also a member of the Robotics team in my school. Joining various state and national level competition allowed me to build robots with various functions to overcome the challenges given. This helped me develop analytical and creative thinking skills while getting exposure to engineering technology and knowledge. We used our knowledge on sensors and floatation to produce a fully functional robot while applying knowledge on motors and gears to build a strong fighting robot. I have also learnt the importance of communication, cooperation and tolerance to move and succeed as a team from all the competitions that I participated in.
